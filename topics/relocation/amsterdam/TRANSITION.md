### Furnished vs unfurnished

| Type | What's included | Price premium | Best for |
|------|----------------|--------------|----------|

### Rental process — what you need to prepare

**Document dossier (have ready as a single PDF BEFORE you start viewing):**
- [ ] Valid passport (you + wife)
- [ ] Employment contract from Delphyr (permanent = huge advantage)
- [ ] Last 3 payslips OR employer statement (new hire)
- [ ] Last 3 months bank statements
- [ ] Residence permit (your LTR card)
- [ ] BSN or proof of BSN application
- [ ] Previous landlord reference (verhuurdersverklaring) if available

**Income rule:** Gross monthly income must be 3-4x rent. Combined couple income counts.
Example: €2,000 rent → need €6,000–8,000 gross/month combined.

**Timeline:** Well-priced apartments rent in under 1 week. Listings get 50+ applications in 24h. Budget 2-8 weeks for search.

**Deposit (borg):** 1-2 months rent. Photo-document everything at move-in.

### Platforms & search strategy

**Must-use:**
- [pararius.nl](https://pararius.nl) — largest rental portal, English available. Get Pararius+ for instant alerts.
- [funda.nl](https://funda.nl) — biggest Dutch property site (use browser for English)

**Expat-friendly:**
- [housinganywhere.com](https://housinganywhere.com) — furnished, remote search possible

**Alert tools (speed is everything in this market):**

**Strategy:**
1. Set alerts on ALL platforms before arriving

### Key rental terms

| Dutch | English | Note |
|-------|---------|------|
| Borg | Security deposit | 1-2 months, get terms in writing |
| Huurcontract | Lease | — |
| Onbepaalde tijd (Model A) | Indefinite lease | STRONGEST rights. Push for this. |
| Tijdelijk contract (Model B) | Temporary lease, max 2 years | Ends automatically. No extension. Avoid if possible. |
| Servicekosten | Service charges | Check what's included vs excluded |
| VvE | Homeowners association | Monthly fee for building upkeep |
| Opzegtermijn | Notice period | Usually 1 month for tenant |
| Diplomatenclausule | Diplomatic clause | Allows early termination if leaving NL. Negotiate this in. |
| Huurcommissie | Rent tribunal | Can challenge unfair rent (process in Dutch only) |
| Inschrijven | Address registration | MUST be possible. Some landlords refuse = red flag. |

### Points system (puntenwoning) — know this

Every apartment gets scored on size, facilities, energy label, etc.:
- ≤143 points: social housing (€933 max, waitlists, not for expats)
- 144-186 points: mid-rent, partially regulated, rent capped
- 187+ points: free market, no cap

If you find a mid-rent property, check the points — you could save hundreds/month. You can request the calculation from the landlord. Challenge via Huurcommissie if overpriced.

### Pitfalls to avoid

- **Never pay deposit before viewing in person** — scam epidemic in Amsterdam
- **Fake listings** — scammers copy real Funda photos. Verify agent identity.
- **Agent fees** — by law, if landlord hires the agent, landlord pays. Don't let them charge you too.
- **Temporary contracts** — Model B max 2 years, then you're out. Push for indefinite (Model A).
- **Registration blocked** — if landlord won't let you register the address, walk away. Without registration = no BSN = no bank = no insurance.
- **Hidden defects** — check heating, hot water, mold, ventilation, elevator. Visit in person.
- **Probation period** — if you're in proeftijd at Delphyr, get an employer confirmation letter to reassure landlords.

### 2. Dutch Banking

**Best banks for newcomers:**
- **bunq** — Fastest to open, fully English app, online-only. Get started in days. €10/month for Easy Banker.
- **ING** — Largest, good English, full branch network
- **ABN AMRO** — Strong expat services
- **Rabobank** — Cooperative, community feel

**Required to open account:**
- Valid passport
- BSN number
- Proof of address (rental agreement or employment contract)

**Joint account:** "en/of" (either can withdraw) or "en/en" (both required). bunq makes this easy in-app.

### 3. Dutch Healthcare

**Basic insurance (basisverzekering):** Mandatory. ~€120–150/month per person.
- Covers: GP, hospital, medication, mental health, physiotherapy (limited)

**Providers:** CZ, VGZ, Zilveren Kruis, Menzis, Ditzo

**Supplemental (aanvullend):** Optional. ~€10–50/month for dental, physiotherapy, alternative.

**Compare at:** independer.nl

**GP (huisarts):** Register at a practice near your home. Use zorgkaartnederland.nl to find.

### 4. 30% Tax Ruling

**What:** 30% of your salary is tax-free for up to 5 years (tax-free reimbursement of extraterritorial costs).

**Who qualifies:** Foreign specialists with specific expertise, salary ≥~€40,000/year, recruited from outside NL.

**How to apply:** Employer submits application to Belastingdienst. Takes 4–8 weeks.

**Pitfalls:**
- Not automatic — must be applied for
- 5-year max, non-renewable
- Some employers don't offer it
- Cannot backdate
- Clarify whether it's applied to gross salary or as net supplement

### 5. Dutch Bureaucracy Essentials

**Municipality registration (inschrijving):** Register within 5 days of arriving at your local gemeente.

**Documents needed:**

**BSN:** Received at registration. Essential for: banking, taxes, health insurance, employment.

### 10. Dutch Work Culture

**Hours:** 36–40 hours/week standard. Flexible (glijdend werken) common.

**Communication:**
- Direct, pragmatic, low-hierarchy
- English widely spoken in tech
- "Say what you mean" — not rude, just efficient

**Hierarchy:** Flat in startups. First-name basis. Consensus-driven (poldermodel).

**Holidays:**
- ~25 paid days/year (legal minimum)
- Dutch public holidays only (not US/other)
- Summer: most Dutch take July–August off

**Work-life balance:** Strong. Emails after hours often discouraged. Tech startups vary — more startup energy but improving.
