# Glassdoor Research

Saved: 2026-04-06

## Bottom line

The Finom Glassdoor material is useful mostly for process and culture signals, not for predicting exact Ivo questions.

The clearest directional takeaways are:
- interview loops can be lean, fast, and a bit inconsistent
- some candidates describe poor follow-up or process roughness
- content signals still point toward practical engineering judgment, product collaboration, and technical strategy rather than abstract puzzle interviews

## How to use this note

Use these entries as:
- weak signals about hiring process quality
- moderate signals about startup culture and expectations
- light directional hints about discussion topics

Do not use them as:
- a literal forecast of your interview script
- strong evidence for how Ivo personally interviews

## Cross-entry patterns

### Process signals

- some loops appear fast and simple
- some candidates report home tasks or extra assignments
- at least two negative reports point to process roughness or weak communication

### Content signals

- practical engineering questions show up
- design / module-thinking shows up
- product collaboration and tech strategy show up at leadership level

### Cultural interpretation

Best working assumption:
- Finom likely runs like a scaling startup rather than a polished big-company hiring machine
- expect useful conversations, but do not expect perfect process hygiene

## Practical takeaway for interview 2

For the Ivo round, the useful read is:
- be ready for a high-level conversation that still expects concrete engineering judgment
- sound pragmatic, low-ego, and execution-oriented
- avoid overfitting to any one Glassdoor entry

## Caveats

- Glassdoor entries are self-reported and noisy
- some entries are clearly more reliable for process than for question content
- this note should inform expectations, not drive the prep narrative
