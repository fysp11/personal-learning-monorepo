# Slide Deck: Human Review, Escalation & Safe Boundaries

Create a **7-slide** interview-prep presentation for **myself** on when medical AI should help, refuse, narrow the task, or hand off to a human.

## Slides

1. **Title: Human Review, Escalation & Safe Boundaries**
   - How safe medical AI refuses, narrows, and hands off

2. **When the System Should Stop**
   - Weak or missing evidence
   - Contradictions or implausible values
   - Diagnosis, prognosis, prescribing, treatment planning
   - Good phrase: better to stop than bluff in medicine

3. **Low-Risk vs High-Risk Workflow Examples**
   - Lower risk: chart summary, guideline lookup, correspondence drafting
   - Higher risk: diagnosis suggestions, treatment plans, dose changes
   - Use this slide as a memory aid for where autonomy can and cannot grow

4. **Abstain, Narrow, or Escalate**
   - **Abstain** when support is too weak
   - **Narrow** when the query is too broad or inferential
   - **Escalate** when the request is high-risk or out of scope
   - Mini example: narrow “What’s going on with this patient?” into a safer retrieval question

5. **How Review Fits Into Workflow**
   - Route to clinician or nurse review queue when needed
   - Review drafts before EHR entry for sensitive outputs
   - Keep an audit trail of why the system refused or escalated

6. **Why This Matters in Delphyr-Style Integration**
   - Delphyr is integration-first, not “another screen”
   - Safe assistance should fit into existing EHR/HIS habits, not create a parallel workflow
   - Human review is part of the product boundary, not a late add-on

7. **What I’d Say in the Interview**
   - “Human-in-the-loop is not a patch; it’s part of the architecture.”
   - “I’d automate low-risk retrieval and documentation first, then escalate higher-risk clinical reasoning.”

## Style
- Practical, workflow-oriented, and easy to say out loud
- Use concrete examples more than abstract policy language
- Keep the focus on decision boundaries and handoff logic
- Optimize for personal recall during the interview
