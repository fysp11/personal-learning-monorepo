# Delphyr — Recruiter Feedback for Samuel

## Summary for Samuel

### How the call went
The introductory call with Delphyr went very well — about 30 minutes of genuine technical exchange, not a formal interrogation. The interviewer was engaged, asked good probing questions, and shared substantial detail about the company, team, and roadmap. The overall tone was collaborative and mutually exploratory.

### What landed well
1. **Self-healing scraper agent story** — described building an autonomous agent that detects broken scrapers via Sentry, analyzes the new site structure, proposes fixes, tests them, and deploys — saving ~20 hours/week of engineering time. The interviewer reacted with a genuine "Amazing."
2. **Evaluation framework depth** — explained a granular, action-level evaluation approach for agent systems (not just binary pass/fail). This clearly resonated with their clinical accuracy needs.
3. **Commit/rollback pattern for clinical AI** — proposed a transactional model for agent actions in healthcare: actions are staged, reviewed, and committed or rolled back. Drew an analogy to database transactions and clinical trials. This mapped directly to their safety requirements.
4. **Privacy-first thinking** — mentioning that my wife is a privacy/GDPR specialist showed genuine (not performative) awareness of data protection. For a medical data company, this landed well.
5. **DSPy recommendation** — mentioned this self-improving prompt optimization library, which got a "that's pretty cool" reaction and showed mutual technical curiosity.

- **Team**: Small (~6 people) — 2 senior engineers/architects, 1 mid-senior data scientist, junior data engineers. High-trust, high-impact per person.
- **Growth coming**: Expecting significant workload increase in the coming 6 months. They need someone soon.
- **Decision speed**: "We tend to take decisions quickly."
